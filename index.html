about

how do we understand ourselves better, connect more meaningfully, and design systems (tech, personal, social) that support human flourishing?

still looking for answers:) currently building in AI for wellness, personal growth, and connection, grounded in a decade of product work and research in AI and previously in electronics. i've commercialized research twice, shipped products to 30M+ users.

i've also spent equal time building infrastructure for others: led MIT's Brazil incubator, established hackathons and accelerators in Kazakhstan, brought startup ecosystems to underserved regions.

the pattern: notice what's missing, explore it deeply, build it, then open the path for others. the work is as much about wellbeing and community as it is about code and metrics.

the project proposed a larger study to understand anxiety signatures in real-world contexts, moving beyond lab settings to capture authentic emotional responses.

interface design patterns & biofeedback research
for Martin Bechthold's class at Harvard Graduate School of Design

designed variations of a language learning app to study how different UI/UX patterns influence learner performance and content recall.

used biofeedback sensors to measure physiological responses while users interacted with different interface designs, identifying which patterns enhanced focus versus caused distraction.

the research explored evidence-based approaches to educational interface design, connecting cognitive load theory with measurable user responses.
