about

i spend a lot of time observing human behavior - how people think, learn, and adapt, and exploring how we can build technology that genuinely helps them grow.

i grew up in Kazakhstan, in a family of academics who made a lot work with a little. focus on learning and personal growth opened a lot of doors for me, and community was always the thing that held you when the doors were slow to open.

i've spent the last decade working at the intersection of personal growth, communities, technology, and behavior change. AI research at MIT Media Lab (commercialized, NSF-funded, deployed at Cornell Med). voice agents and AI personalization shipped to CVS and Blue Shield. engagement systems at Andrew Ng's Workera. alongside all of it, building infrastructure for others: the largest hackathon in Kazakhstan, social impact incubators across Central Asia, MIT's Brazil healthtech accelerator, a book club in SF.

currently tinkering with Daily — a personal knowledge platform for the scattered attention era, with community at the center.
